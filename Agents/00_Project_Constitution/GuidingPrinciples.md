# Guiding Principles

Status: Approved

## GP-001 — Build the mental model

The intended path is:

notation → mental model → performance

The notation should become less necessary as the learner understands structure, harmony, patterns, timing, and arrangement identity.

## GP-002 — Represent intent before mechanical expansion

Prefer direct representation of harmony, bass movement, accompaniment behavior, rhythmic identity, reusable patterns, voicing intent, primary lines, and arrangement-defining ideas. Exact notes remain supported primitives.

## GP-003 — Use adaptive density

Simple passages should remain visually simple. Arrangement-critical or genuinely complex passages may contain more detail.

## GP-004 — Preserve repetition as repetition

Repeated music should remain recognizably related in both the model and the rendering. Variations and alternate endings should expose differences without disguising the common source.

## GP-005 — Treat measures as coordinates

Measures and beats provide reliable temporal coordinates. They are not mandatory cognitive or practice boundaries.

## GP-006 — Separate musical roles from hands

Harmony, bass, accompaniment, primary line, and rhythm are musical functions. Left and right hand are execution assignments. A role may cross hands, and a hand may serve multiple roles.

## GP-007 — Use canonical musical terminology

Use established musical names such as Alberti bass, walking bass, and stride. Song-specific names may be examples or aliases only.

## GP-008 — Support progressive disclosure

The same canonical representation should support views that reveal harmony, melody or primary line, bass, accompaniment, voicings, embellishments, articulation, and dynamics progressively.

## GP-009 — Preserve transposable knowledge

Changing key should preserve learned form, relationships, patterns, role structure, repetitions, and arrangement identity.

## GP-010 — Teach through familiar knowledge without corrupting analysis

Pedagogical overlays may connect unfamiliar ideas to familiar shapes. For example, `Am7` may display “think C/A” as an optional exact pitch-class-set hint. The canonical analysis remains `Am7`.
