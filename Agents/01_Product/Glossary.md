# Glossary

**Arrangement** — A particular realization of a song, including voicing, bass, accompaniment, fills, articulation, and other identity-bearing choices. It does not own recipes, experiments, learning plans, or learning chunks.

**Arrangement capability profile** — Evidence derived only from a validated canonical or normalized arrangement. It cannot claim that a learning plan, renderer feature, or experiment exists.

**Canonical representation** — Authoritative semantic music data independent of any visual view or pedagogical plan.

**Chord-quality reference** — Controlled, versioned semantic identifier for chord quality. Display labels and aliases are derived and are not semantic identity.

**Compatibility input** — Composite of separately authoritative arrangement, optional verified learning-plan, strategy, renderer, and environment capability profiles.

**Essentiality** — Degree to which an event or idea is required for recognition or a convincing performance.

**Experiment definition** — Authored reproducible comparison specification identifying fixtures, treatment recipes, controlled and changed variables, tasks, observations, and status.

**Experiment run manifest** — Immutable automated evidence identifying exact inputs, versions, hashes, diagnostics, and output hashes. It does not contain or imply human learning results.

**Familiar-shape hint** — Optional pedagogical restatement of a chord as a familiar upper structure over a bass, such as `Am7` shown with `C/A`. It does not replace canonical analysis.

**Golden corpus** — Permanent set of representative lawful examples used for design, regression, vocabulary, and learning validation.

**Harmonic analysis annotation** — Non-authoritative free text, optionally identifying an external analysis system. Semantic code may not depend on it.

**Harmonic roadmap** — Reduced view emphasizing form, chords, beat placement, repetition, and major variations.

**Learning chunk** — Derived pedagogical practice unit inside a learning plan. It references canonical content and may differ by role or hand; it is not owned by the arrangement.

**Learning plan** — Reproducible derived artifact produced by a learning transformation and containing chunks, relationships, provenance, and diagnostics that reference one canonical arrangement.

**Learning-plan capability profile** — Evidence derived from a verified learning plan and its matching arrangement hash.

**Learning transformation** — Reusable, versioned, deterministic pedagogical strategy that derives a learning plan from a compatible arrangement.

**Musical idea** — Reusable cognitive and structural unit whose boundary follows meaningful musical change rather than fixed measure count.

**Pattern** — Named or user-defined reusable musical behavior with parameters and explicit overrides.

**Primary line** — Currently most salient line, which may be vocal melody, instrumental melody, riff, bass hook, or another defining line.

**Representation recipe** — Versioned declarative configuration selecting compatible presentation strategies without containing musical content or executable code.

**Role** — Musical function such as harmony, bass, accompaniment, primary line, or rhythm.

**Slash bass** — Specified bass note independent of the upper harmony. It does not by itself define upper voicing or inversion.

**Specificity state** — Required, suggested, optional, intentionally unspecified, or unknown/not entered.

**Structural chunk** — Unit belonging to musical form, such as section, phrase, idea, or transition.

**Treatment** — Reproducible application of one resolved representation recipe to canonical/normalized input. A treatment is experimental and is not the final notation system.

**Variation** — Material derived from known material with explicit differences in ending, rhythm, harmony, register, fill, voicing, or another parameter.

**Voicing** — Selected pitches, register, spacing, doubling, and omissions used to realize harmony.
