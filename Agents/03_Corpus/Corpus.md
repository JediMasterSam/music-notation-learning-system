# Golden Corpus

Status: Approved requirements; selections partly open.

## Purpose

The corpus is the permanent regression suite and design arbiter. It must expose where a model, pattern, renderer, transformation, recipe, or vocabulary succeeds or fails.

## Required categories

| Corpus ID | Category | Required behavior |
|---|---|---|
| C-G01 | Melody only | Exact notes without invented harmony; same source can drive multiple treatments |
| C-G02 | Melody plus harmony | Temporal alignment between line and chords |
| C-G03 | Chord-driven pop | Form, harmonic roadmap, repeated accompaniment |
| C-G04 | Independent hands | Role and hand separation |
| C-G05 | Voicing or inversion critical | Explicit inversion, slash bass, upper voicing, and controlled chord quality |
| C-G06 | Repeated accompaniment | Pattern and variation handling |
| C-G07 | Classical or baroque | Polyphonic or pattern-rich historical material |
| C-G08 | Ragtime or stride | Strong left-hand pattern and syncopation |
| C-G09 | Familiar-shape hint | Canonical chord plus optional pedagogical reframing |
| C-G10 | Learning transformation reuse | One transformation definition applied to multiple compatible arrangements |
| C-G11 | Treatment compatibility | Supported, limited, incompatible, and unavailable recipe cases with artifact-scoped evidence |

## Style coverage

Pilot pool should eventually include jazz, stride, ragtime, baroque, classical, romantic, chord-driven popular music, and public-domain works with strong genealogical connections to modern popular music.

## Source policy

Every item must record:

- title or fixture name;
- composer or source;
- public-domain, licensed, user-supplied, synthetic, or analytical-fixture status;
- permitted repository usage;
- source reference;
- musical and workbench behaviors tested;
- limitations.

## Selection rule

Do not select music merely because it is easy to encode. Select examples that force distinct product requirements to prove themselves. Automated fixture success does not establish learner benefit or corpus completeness.
