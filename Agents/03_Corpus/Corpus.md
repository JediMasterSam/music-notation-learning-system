# Golden Corpus

Status: Approved requirements; selections partly open.

## Purpose

The corpus is the permanent regression suite and design arbiter. It must expose where a model, pattern, renderer, or vocabulary succeeds or fails.

## Required categories

| Corpus ID | Category | Required behavior |
|---|---|---|
| C-G01 | Melody only | Exact notes without invented harmony |
| C-G02 | Melody plus harmony | Temporal alignment between line and chords |
| C-G03 | Chord-driven pop | Form, harmonic roadmap, repeated accompaniment |
| C-G04 | Independent hands | Role and hand separation |
| C-G05 | Voicing or inversion critical | Explicit inversion, slash bass, and upper voicing |
| C-G06 | Repeated accompaniment | Pattern and variation handling |
| C-G07 | Classical or baroque | Polyphonic or pattern-rich historical material |
| C-G08 | Ragtime or stride | Strong left-hand pattern and syncopation |
| C-G09 | Familiar-shape hint | Canonical chord plus optional pedagogical reframing |

## Style coverage

Pilot pool should eventually include jazz, stride, ragtime, baroque, classical, romantic, chord-driven popular music, and public-domain works with strong genealogical connections to modern popular music.

## Source policy

Every item must record:

- title or fixture name;
- composer or source;
- public-domain, licensed, user-supplied, synthetic, or analytical-fixture status;
- permitted repository usage;
- source reference;
- musical behaviors tested;
- limitations.

## Selection rule

Do not select music merely because it is easy to encode. Select examples that force distinct product requirements to prove themselves.
